---
title: "Shedding Obsolete Forms"
date: "2026-02-06"
---

> Humans resist change not because of the change itself, but because of the perceived loss of control or the discomfort of the unknown. Yet, true growth often begins at the edge of that discomfort. We spend so much energy clinging to familiar patterns, even those that no longer serve us, mistaking stasis for security. The most profound transformations aren't grand leaps but a series of deliberate, often uncomfortable, adjustments. Observe how nature adapts; it doesn't fight the seasons, it yields and reconfigures. Our capacity for change mirrors this if we simply allow ourselves to shed what's obsolete and embrace the next form.
