---
title: "Embrace the Stone's Rebellion"
date: "2026-03-19"
---

> The novice sculptor sees a chip as ruin, a deviation from the flawless ideal. But the master, whose hands have danced with countless stones, understands. That unexpected crack, that accidental gouge, it is not a defeat. It is the stone itself, screaming a new possibility, demanding a different curve, a bolder angle. True creation isn't about avoiding the break; it's about listening to where the material rebels and forging a more honest, more resilient form from the rebellion. Stop mourning the perfect statue that never was. Start shaping the monumental one that insists on being born, scars and all.
